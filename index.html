A Decade of Degrees

Universities are constantly changing and Northwestern is no exception. Its history—old and new—is written in the creation, destruction, and changing popularity of its majors.

When the University opened on November 5, 1855 it had five departments and two degrees. Today there are TK different majors offered.

After World War I two new majors were introduced: Military Science and Physical Education and Hygiene. To be prepared for wartime jobs during World War II more Northwestern students studied math, physics and chemistry.

The period after the war saw a huge change in Northwestern's curriculum. New majors in Naval Science and Home Economics were created. In light of the struggle against communism, classes in Western Civilization to teach democratic values grew in popularity. This was accompanied by a renewed emphasis on the sciences, another intellectual battlefield against the Soviet Union. In 1971, the Department of Computer Science was created. In 1972, the African-American Studies department was established.

But change is not just a thing of the past; the University is still evolving. Over the last ten years the populartiy of majors has continuted to change.
